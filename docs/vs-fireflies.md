## The "Bot in Meeting" Problem

### Corporate Policies

Many companies have policies against:
- Unknown participants in meetings
- Recording to third-party services
- Bots in confidential calls

When Fireflies bot joins, it may:
- Get kicked by IT security
- Raise compliance concerns
- Make clients uncomfortable
- Violate NDAs

### Client Relationships

Imagine this call:

> **You:** "Hi Client, let's discuss your legal matter."
>
> **System:** "Fireflies.ai Notetaker has joined the meeting."
>
> **Client:** "What is that? You're sending this to a third party?"

**With Private Transcript:**

You record locally. Client never knows unless you tell them. Audio stays on your machine.

## When to Choose Fireflies

Fireflies makes sense if:
- You want fully automated meeting recording
- Privacy isn't a concern
- Participants are OK with a bot
- You need deep CRM integration
- Your company allows recording bots

## When to Choose Private Transcript

Private Transcript is better if:
- You handle confidential information
- Clients would be uncomfortable with a bot
- Your company blocks third-party bots
- You want to own your recordings
- You prefer one-time payment
- You need offline capability

---

## The Bottom Line

**Fireflies** trades privacy for convenience. The bot joins automatically, but everyone knows you're recording, and the audio goes to their servers.

**Private Transcript** keeps you in control. You decide when to record, the audio stays local, and no one has to know.

For casual internal meetings, Fireflies is fine. For anything professional, confidential, or client-facing, Private Transcript is the safer choice.
